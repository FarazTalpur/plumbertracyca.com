Frequently Asked Questions

The size of the water heater depends on the number of people in the household and daily water usage. For a family of 1-2 people, a 30-40 gallon tank is usually sufficient. For larger households, a 50-80 gallon tank may be necessary. It's best to calculate your peak hour demand and choose a tank with a capacity that meets or exceeds that amount.

There are several types of water heaters, including tankless, storage tank, heat pump, solar, and condensing water heaters. Each type has its own advantages and is suitable for different needs. For example, tankless units provide hot water on demand and are more energy-efficient, whereas storage tank models are more affordable and have a larger capacity.

The installation of a new water heater typically takes between 2 to 4 hours, depending on the complexity of the job and whether or not you need to remove an old unit. Factors such as the type of water heater, the location of installation, and any additional plumbing or electrical work needed can also affect the installation time.

In most areas, a permit is required to install a water heater. This helps ensure the work meets local building codes and safety standards. It's important to check with your local building department to understand the specific requirements in your area.

Regular maintenance of your water heater can extend its life and improve efficiency. This includes flushing the tank annually to remove sediment buildup, inspecting the anode rod for corrosion every 2-3 years, and checking for leaks or signs of wear. Additionally, temperature and pressure relief valves should be tested periodically to ensure they function correctly.
