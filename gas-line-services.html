Frequently Asked Questions

If you smell gas, immediately evacuate the premises. Do not use electronics or open flames. Once you are safely outside, call your gas company or local emergency services to report the suspected leak.

It's recommended to have your gas lines inspected and serviced annually by a qualified professional to ensure they are in good condition and to prevent potential hazards.

Common signs of a gas leak include a rotten egg smell, dead or dying vegetation in an otherwise green area, hissing sounds near a gas appliance, or a white cloud or dust cloud near a gas line.

Installing a gas line requires specialized knowledge and skills and should only be done by a licensed professional. Improper installation can lead to dangerous leaks and violations of local building codes.
