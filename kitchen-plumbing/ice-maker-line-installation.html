Frequently Asked Questions

The recommended distance between the water source and the ice maker should not exceed 10 feet. This ensures optimal water pressure and efficient ice production.

A 1/4-inch diameter water line is typically recommended for ice maker installations to ensure adequate water flow.

To connect an ice maker line to an existing water supply, you will need to install a saddle valve or a dedicated shut-off valve near the cold water supply line. Then, connect the appropriate fittings to ensure a tight seal.

It is recommended to use copper tubing or braided stainless steel tubing for ice maker connections due to their durability and ability to handle the water pressure.

While some people may be able to install an ice maker line themselves, it is recommended to hire a professional if you are inexperienced with plumbing to avoid any potential water damage or improper installation.
